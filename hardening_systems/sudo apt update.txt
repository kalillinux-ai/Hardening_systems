-sudo apt update	

Refreshes the local package index. It contacts the software repositories on the internet and downloads the latest list of available packages and their version numbers.

-sudo apt upgrade	

Installs the updates. It compares 

your currently installed packages against the recently updated local list (from the update command) and downloads/installs the newer versions.


why use it ? 

"sudo apt update" is the first and most crucial step in managing or upgrading software on debian-based or ubuntu.

it performs the task of synchronizing your local package index with the official package repositories on the internet.


"sudo apt upgrade" is essential for: 

 Security: software updates have security patches that fix vulnerabilities hackers could exploit. keep packages up to date helps protect your system 

bug fixes: updates fix software bugs and enhance overall system performance
